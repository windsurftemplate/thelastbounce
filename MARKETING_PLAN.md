# The Last Bounce - Detailed Marketing Plan

## Executive Summary
Launch strategy for The Last Bounce, a premium lifetime tennis ball product. Target: 1,000 units sold in first 90 days, generating $29,990 in revenue.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-2)
**Goal: Build anticipation and collect 500 email addresses**

### Actions:
1. **Landing Page Optimization**
   - Install Facebook Pixel and Google Analytics
   - Add email capture popup: "Be first to get The Last Bounce - 20% early bird discount"
   - Create countdown timer: "Launch in X days"
   - A/B test two headlines

2. **Social Media Setup**
   - Create Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Twitter accounts
   - Post content: 2x daily
   - Content types:
     - Behind-the-scenes product development
     - Tennis ball waste statistics (shocking facts)
     - Teaser videos of bounce tests
     - User pain points (dead ball frustrations)

3. **Influencer Outreach**
   - Identify 20 micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) in tennis niche
   - Send personalized DMs offering free product for review
   - Target: Tennis coaches, club pros, tennis content creators
   - Budget: $0 (product seeding only)

4. **PR Preparation**
   - Write press release: "Revolutionary Tennis Ball Promises Lifetime Bounce"
   - Create press kit with high-res images and founder story
   - List target publications: Tennis Magazine, Tennis.com, sports blogs

### Metrics:
- Email signups: 500
- Social followers: 1,000 combined
- Influencer commitments: 5

---

## Phase 2: Launch (Weeks 3-4)
**Goal: Generate first 100 sales, create social proof**

### Actions:
1. **Email Campaign**
   - Day 1: "We're Live! 20% off for early supporters"
   - Day 3: Social proof email with first customer testimonials
   - Day 7: Scarcity email "Limited first batch"
   - Subject line testing for >30% open rate

2. **Paid Advertising - Testing Phase**
   - **Facebook/Instagram Ads**: $500 budget
     - Audience: Ages 25-55, interest in tennis, active lifestyle
     - Creative: Video showing ball bounce comparison (old vs. TLB)
     - Target ROAS: 3:1 ($1,500 revenue)

   - **Google Ads**: $300 budget
     - Search terms: "best tennis balls", "tennis balls that last", "premium tennis balls"
     - Target CPA: $15

3. **Influencer Launch**
   - 5 influencers post unboxing/review videos
   - Provide unique discount codes for tracking
   - Offer $5 commission per sale

4. **Reddit/Forum Marketing**
   - Participate authentically in r/tennis, tenniswarehouse forums
   - Share value first, mention product naturally
   - Do AMA: "I created a tennis ball that never dies, AMA"

5. **Retargeting Campaign**
   - Setup Facebook/Google retargeting for site visitors
   - Show testimonials and guarantee to warm leads
   - Budget: $200

### Metrics:
- Sales: 100 units ($2,999 revenue)
- Website traffic: 5,000 visitors
- Conversion rate: 2%
- Email open rate: >30%
- Social engagement rate: >5%

---

## Phase 3: Scale (Weeks 5-8)
**Goal: Reach 400 sales, optimize ad performance**

### Actions:
1. **Scale Profitable Ads**
   - Double budget on ads with ROAS >3:1
   - Create lookalike audiences from purchasers
   - Test new creative angles:
     - Environmental angle: "Stop wasting tennis balls"
     - Financial angle: "Save $400+ per year"
     - Performance angle: "Consistency breeds champions"

2. **Content Marketing**
   - Launch blog: SEO-optimized articles
     - "Why Do Tennis Balls Go Dead? The Science Explained"
     - "How Much Money Do You Waste on Tennis Balls?"
     - "Environmental Impact of Tennis Ball Waste"
   - Target: 1,000 organic visitors/month

3. **YouTube Strategy**
   - Create product demo video
   - Sponsor 2-3 tennis YouTube channels
   - Budget: $500 per sponsorship
   - Run YouTube pre-roll ads: $400 budget

4. **Amazon Launch**
   - List on Amazon for additional channel
   - Use Fulfilled by Amazon for convenience
   - Leverage Amazon reviews for credibility

5. **Email Nurture Sequence**
   - 7-email sequence for non-buyers
   - Educational content + social proof
   - Final email: Limited-time discount

6. **User-Generated Content Campaign**
   - Encourage customers to post videos with #LastBounceChallenge
   - Repost best content
   - Monthly giveaway for best video

### Metrics:
- Sales: 400 units ($11,996 revenue)
- Ad ROAS: >4:1
- Organic traffic: 1,000/month
- Customer reviews: >50
- Average rating: >4.5 stars

---

## Phase 4: Momentum (Weeks 9-12)
**Goal: Reach 500+ sales, build sustainable growth engine**

### Actions:
1. **Referral Program**
   - Launch "Share the Bounce" program
   - Give $10 credit for each referral
   - Referred friend gets $10 off
   - Promote heavily to existing customers

2. **Partnership Development**
   - Approach tennis clubs for bulk orders
   - Offer club partnerships: "Official ball of [Club Name]"
   - 20% discount on orders >50 cans
   - Target: 10 club partnerships

3. **PR Push**
   - Send press kit to 50 publications
   - Pitch unique angles: sustainability, innovation, cost savings
   - Goal: 3 media features

4. **Expansion of Ad Channels**
   - LinkedIn Ads: Target tennis club managers, coaches
   - Pinterest Ads: Visual content for sports enthusiasts
   - TikTok Ads: Short-form viral content
   - Budget: $300 per channel

5. **Customer Success Stories**
   - Interview happy customers
   - Create case study videos
   - Feature on website and in ads
   - Build email campaign around stories

6. **Holiday Campaign** (if timing aligns)
   - "Perfect Gift for Tennis Lovers"
   - Gift packaging option
   - Holiday-specific ads and emails

### Metrics:
- Sales: 500+ units ($14,995+ revenue)
- Total revenue: $29,990+
- Customer acquisition cost: <$20
- Lifetime value: $35+ (including referrals)
- Email list: 2,000+
- Monthly organic traffic: 2,500+

## Risk Mitigation

### Potential Challenges:
1. **Low initial conversion rates**
   - Solution: A/B test landing page elements weekly
   - Offer money-back guarantee prominently
   - Add live chat support

2. **High CAC**
   - Solution: Focus on organic and referral growth
   - Improve landing page conversion rate
   - Create viral content

3. **Skepticism about "lifetime" claim**
   - Solution: Extensive social proof
   - Video testimonials from long-term users
   - Transparent warranty terms

4. **Competition response**
   - Solution: Build strong brand and community quickly
   - Focus on unique value prop and customer service
   - File for patent protection
